Dragonite is known as Kairyu in Japanese, a term that signifies a 'cheerful dragon'. Provided by: Fandom How does Dragonite differ from its pre-evolved forms? Dragonite, an orange dragon pokemon, differs from its pre-evolved forms, Dratini and Dragonair, in appearance and behavior. Unlike the Eastern dragon-like, limbless, wingless, blue Dratini and Dragonair, Dragonite resembles a European dragon, being large, bipedal, and having two turquoise wings. Known for their warmhearted and selfless nature, Dragonite often rescue those lost at sea, but can become aggressive if their environment is threatened. They are also incredibly swift, able to circle the globe in 16 hours. Provided by: Fandom What type of Pokemon is Dragonite? Dragonite, or Kairyuu in Japanese, is a Pseudo-Legendary Pokémon with a Dragon/Flying type. First introduced in Generation I, this Pokémon is known for its challenging capture and training process, as well as its late evolution. Dragonite stands out due to its superior stats compared to other Pokémon types. Provided by: Fandom Is Dragonite considered a legendary Pokemon? Dragonite, the orange dragon Pokémon, is not classified as a legendary Pokémon. It holds the status of a pseudo-legendary Pokémon due to its high base stats and three-stage evolution process. It's noteworthy for being the first pseudo-legendary Pokémon caught in its fully evolved form by a main character. Provided by: Fandom What similarities does Dragonite share with Charizard? Dragonite and Charizard are similar in many ways. Both have members capable of various move types, including Flying, Water, Fire, and Dragon-type moves. They have members that evolved from one Type to a dual Type, and both have fully evolved Flying, Ground, and Normal types. Additionally, they share an orange color and blue underside of their wings. However, Dragonite has a rounder, friendlier appearance and lacks a flame on its tail. Provided by: Fandom Biology [ ] Physiology [ ] Concept artwork of Lance's Dragonite . Dragonite greatly differ from their sea serpent-like pre-evolved forms. Both Dratini and Dragonair had a blue coloring with no limbs or wings (excluding the wing-like ears of Dragonair). Dragonite are large, orange, bipedal dragons with two turquoise wings on its back. Dragonite largely resembles a Charizard , as both have the same orange coloring, and the same blue underside of their wings, except for its lack of a flame on the end of their tails, and a somewhat rounder, and friendlier appearance. Shiny Dragonite's main color is green while the inside of its wings are purple. Dragonite resembles a proper looking European dragon, unlike its pre- evolved forms Dratini and Dragonair , which are more aligned towards Eastern ones. As shown in Pokémon Horizons , Mega Dragonite can perform attacks from the tip of its tail. Behavior [ ] Most Dragonite are incredibly warmhearted as well as selfless and will rescue anyone lost at sea. However, as shown in one episode , they will go on a rampage if their environment is harmed. They tend to battle Kingdra , their sworn rivals, in the wild. Natural abilities [ ] Dragonite are very swift and can circle the globe in just 16 hours. Habitat [ ] Dragonite are very rarely found near water bodies. Some claim that there is an island where only they live, which has only been seen only once . Evolution [ ] Dragonite evolves from Dragonair starting at level 55 and is the final evolution of Dratini . Dragonite can Mega Evolve into Mega Dragonite using its Dragoninite. Ult's Mega Dragonite. 1/8 Dragonite made its first appearance in the episode Mystery at the Lighthouse , where Bill was searching for it. It appeared during the last moments of the episode and was unusually large, making this Dragonite in particular a Giant Pokémon . Dragonite have also made appearances in other episodes under the ownership of trainers including Lance and Drake . Lance's cousin, Clair also owns a Dragonite which originally belongs to the very first Gym Leader of Blackthorn City. In The Mastermind of Mirage Pokémon , Professor Oak used his own Dragonite in battle against the Mirage Master. Dragonite also made an appearance in Pokémon: The First Movie as Mewtwo's "postman" as it uses Dragonite to send the invitations out. Iris has captured a Dragonite in Undella Town when one Dragonite caused all the blackout when its been injured during a battle with a Hydreigon, causing everyone to blame it on him when it was framed. Ash caught a Dragonite in A Test in Paradise! after he bonded with it as a Dragonair and it saved Ash from falling onto Team Rocket's Wailord after evolving from a Dragonite. Dragonite's catchrate in Pokémon Yellow was changed from 45 to 9, but reverted to 45 in all games since. This change is redundant as Dragonite can't be caught in Yellow at all. Dragonite is shorter than its pre-evolution, Dragonair, though this likely only because Dragonair is meaused in body length rather than height. Lance's Dragonite is only level 50 the first time the player character battled him in Johto based games, even though Dragonair doesn't evolve till level 55. All his Dragonite are then also only around the same level, except those found at Dragonspiral Tower . When Lance assists the player character in battling Team Rocket in Mahogany Town his Dragonite is Level 40, 15 levels lower than when Dragonair evolves. Lance's Dragonite also knows Barrier in the original Kanto games, which can't be taught legitimately. The only Dragonite with this ability are those who participated in the 2016 Kanto Classic Wifi Tournament. As an April Fool's Day joke, Expert Gamer magazine claimed that Yoshi (of Mario Bros. fame) was the evolved form of Dragonite. In Pokémon GO, Dragonite is one of the few Pokémon that can have a CP of 3000 or more that is not a Mythical or Legendary . Dragonite is one of the collectable spirits in Super Smash Bros. Ultimate. In the same game, there are files for it being meant as a Poké Ball summon. Dragonite and its evolutionary relatives share their category with the Horsea evolution line members, Salamence, and Kalosian Goodra. All are known as the Dragon Pokémon. Mega Dragonite is the first newly introduced Mega Evolution since the release of Pokémon Omega Ruby and Pokémon Alpha Sapphire , which released 11 years prior. Origin [ ] Dragonite might be based on draconite, a mythical stone found in dragon heads that is pointy. This could also be shown on top of Dragonite's head as it has a pointy horn on top. Dragonite may be based off either Japanese, European or Fairy Dragons (though the first Pokémon with the Fairy-Dragon type combo was actually Altaria ’s Mega Evolution). Its pre evolutions’ Chinese origins remain rooted in its lore as a sea guardian. It heavily resembles dragons found in the artworks done by Utagawa Kuniyoshi, one of the last great masters of the Japanese ukiyo-e style woodblock print. Japanese fans have speculated that the kaiju Daigoro from the 1972 film Daigoro vs. Goliath might have influenced the design of Dragonite based on the fact that Pokémon franchise was actually influenced by the Ultraman franchise where Daigoro vs. Goliath was also produced by Tsuburaya Production. [ 2 ] Mega Dragonite retains the elements of its previous evolution, Dragonair , such as the wing heads and the pearl in its tail, but also invokes angels, more specificialy seraphs, which were described in the Bible as having 6 wings, with the upper wings covering its face, while the bottom wings covers the feet. The wings on its head and under its feet may reference the Greek god Hermes , who his depicted as having wings on his helmet and sandals, with the former heavily used in Japanese pop culture to depict angels, though there more used on succubuses in some depictions. Etymology [ ] Dragonite's name might be a combination of "dragon" and "knight". It may also be a pun on Draconite. Dragonite's Japanese name, Kairyu, literally means "cheerful dragon". Kai may also come from Sea (Since both Cheerful and Sea are represented by the same sound), Kaiju (Monsters like Godzilla, which inspired its pseudo legendary successor Tyranitar ) and Hakai (as it destroys everything when using Outrage ).
