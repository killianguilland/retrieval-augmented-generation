Eevee, a Pokémon with an unstable genetic makeup, can evolve in various ways based on environmental stimuli, including radiation from different stones. This unique characteristic, along with its ability to alter its body composition to match its surroundings, makes Eevee crucial to understanding Pokémon evolution. Eevee stands out as the sole Pokémon with four or more evolutions and was the first to evolve differently based on the time of day. Provided by: Fandom What physical features make Eevee resemble a fox? Eevee, a Pokemon character, bears a resemblance to a fox with its small stature, brown fur, and bushy tail featuring a cream-colored tip. It possesses a catlike muzzle, a small, black, triangular nose, and a fluffy, cream-colored neck ruff. Eevee's round brown eyes and long, rabbit-like ears further contribute to its fox-like appearance. Despite having small paws with three toes and no visible claws, Eevee's overall look is predominantly fox-like. Provided by: Fandom What is the significance of Eevee in the game Pokémon: Let's Go, Eevee!? In Pokémon: Let's Go, Eevee!, Eevee is the player's partner Pokémon, chosen for its popularity and simplicity. Previously, in games set in Kanto, Eevee was only available in the secret entrance of Celadon Mansion. In Let's Go, Eevee was added to Route 17. Additionally, Eevee's cry in games post Pokémon Let's Go is sourced from the anime, a feature once exclusive to Pikachu. Provided by: Fandom What is the Gigantamax form of Eevee and how does it differ from its normal form? In its Gigantamax form, Eevee grows larger with a fluffier, longer ruff and three red clouds encircling its right ear. This form's luxurious fur can captivate foes, making them lose their fighting spirit. Uniquely, Eevee's Gigantamax form is not its final evolutionary stage. Provided by: Fandom Biology [ ] Physiology [ ] Concept art of Eevee Eevee is a small quadrupedal, mammalian creature with fluffy brown fur, that bears feliform and lagomorph traits. Its muzzle is very cat-like, with a small, black and triangular nose. It has a fluffy, cream-colored ruff around its neck similarly to a lion, and a short, brown, bushy tail with a cream-colored tip. Eevee has round, brown eyes, long rabbit-like ears, and pink paw pads on its feet. Its paws are small with three toes and no visible claws. Eevee as a Shiny A Shiny Eevee has silver fur instead of brown, and its ruff and tail-tip are white instead of cream. Its eyes are also gray instead of brown. Eevee is said to have an irregularly-shaped genetic structure, enabling it to evolve into multiple different forms, which depends if it touches a stone or its relationship to their Trainer. As Gigantamax Eevee, its body becomes larger. Its ruff grows longer and fluffier with several triangular furs pointing straight up. The right ear has three red clouds surrounding it in a circle. As the fur becomes fluffier and more luxurious, any enemies caught in it will lose the will to fight due to being enamored by its presence. Eevee's behavior also appears to be more playful and rambunctious when Gigantamaxing, desiring to play with its opponent and often unintentionally crushing them with its size. The Partner Eevee has a lighter shade of brown of its fur, and has unique purple eyes, appearing similar to the Pokémon seen in modern episodes of Pokémon the Series . Special abilities [ ] Eevee are Pokémon with unstable genetic codes. This gives them the ability to evolve into eight different evolved forms triggered by the environment, time of day, friendship levels, or the usage of the evolutionary stones Water Stone , Thunderstone , Fire Stone , Leaf Stone ( Generation VIII ), and Ice Stone (Generation VIII). Partner Eevee is also capable to learning special moves: Bouncy Bubble , Buzzy Buzz , Sizzly Slide , Glitzy Glow , Baddy Bad , Sappy Seed , Freezy Frost , Sparkly Swirl , and the Partner Power, Veevee Volley . Behavior [ ] In " Helping the Hometown Hero! " from the anime, Eevee sometimes greet each other by sniffing the other's tail as done with Sandy and Chloe 's Eevee. In most cases within the franchise, it is known as a happy and docile creature. As its evolutions are all stated to be solitary predatory creatures according to various Pokédex entries, it's likely Eevee is also carnivorous and hunts prey in stealth, similarly to cats or foxes. Although Eevee does not have visible fangs like most of its evolved counterparts, in the Pokémon Adventures manga, they are occasionally depicted. Habitat [ ] Habitat in Paldea in Pokémon Scarlet and Pokémon Violet Eevee is a Pokémon found mainly in cities, although it is likely that they live virtually anywhere in the Pokémon World because they evolve into many different forms to suit their surroundings. It can mostly be found in Kanto but is also seen in Johto , Sinnoh , Unova , Central Kalos , Alola and Galar . However, as with other first partner Pokémon, Eevee is normally extremely rare, as most games only have one specimen available to the player until Generation IV , with some being absent altogether. In Paldea , Eevee (Lv. 30) is most abundant in the grasslands north of Medali . Eevee can also be found around the entrance to the Pokemon League (Lv. 7) northwest of Mesagoza and in the fields west of Cortondo (Lv. 10), although it spawns more rarely here. The League route is particularly unreliable. As mentioned, they tend to spawn near urban settlements, so if you stray too far from town, pull back. If there's still no luck, try leaving and returning to the area, e.g. by flying to a distant town and then straight back to reload the area and reroll the spawns. Another tip is to try eating a Ham Sandwich to increase encounters with Normal-type Pokémon. Eevee usually spawns solo rather than in groups. Gender differences [ ] The female Partner Eevee Eevee did not had a gender difference until Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu! and Pokémon: Let's Go, Eevee! ; A male Partner Eevee has a zigzag pattern at the tip of its tail (which was once for universal genders ). A female Eevee has a rounded, heart-shaped pattern at the tip of its tail. This was likely meant to correspond with Pikachu 's gender differences. Since Pokémon Sword and Pokémon Shield , however, these gender differences apply to all Eevee. Z-Moves [ ] Eevee is one of the few Pokémon in Sun and Moon which have a special Z-Move dedicated to them. The Z-Move Extreme Evoboost is Eevee's signature move . The move is activated by the Z-Crystal , Eevium Z. Its base is Last Resort . Extreme Evoboost can be learned only by an Eevee with Last Resort, and becomes unusable if Last Resort is forgotten or if the Eevee evolves into one of its evolution. Gigantamax [ ] Eevee is one of the few Pokémon in Sword and Shield that can Gigantamax by having a special member from its species. Gigantamax Eevee is the only known Pokémon capable of using the G-Max move , G-Max Cuddle . Evolution [ ] The various evolutions of Eevee Eevee has eight different evolved forms , more than any other Pokémon has. They are often called " Eeveelutions " by fans. Each of Eevee's evolved forms is of a different type . These forms include: Jolteon if it touches a Thunder Stone ; Flareon if it touches a Fire Stone ; Vaporeon if it touches a Water Stone ; Espeon when leveled up with high friendship during the day; Umbreon when leveled up with high friendship during the night; Leafeon when leveled up in Eterna Forest , Pinwheel Forest , Route 20 in Kalos , Petalburg Woods , Lush Jungle or wherever there is a Moss Rock in Generation IV to Generation VII , or if it touches a Leaf Stone in Generation VIII ; Glaceon when leveled up on Route 217 , Twist Mountain , Frost Cavern , Shoal Cave , Mount Lanakila or wherever there is an Ice Rock in Generation IV to Generation VII, or if it touches an Ice Stone in Generation VIII; Sylveon by knowing a Fairy -type move, while leveling up and playing with Eevee through Pokémon-Amie (Gen VI), Pokémon Refresh (Gen VII) and earning at least two affection hearts or when leveled up with high friendship (Generation VIII onward). Origin [ ] An official Eevee plush According to Motofumi Fujiwara , a key person at Game Freak and the creator of the Pokémon, Eevee was based on a vague childhood experience he once had. He described seeing an "indefinable creature" and that Eevee was based on a drawing of all that he could remember of it. Despite its resemblance to certain real-world animals such as foxes or rabbits, it wasn't actually based on any distinct animal, with Fujiwara mentioning that Eevee reminded him of a fluffy cat or dog-like creature. [ 2 ] Each one of Eevee's evolutions are based on the same design but are made to resemble their specific type , effectively making Eevee itself the "blank state". [ 2 ] Etymology [ ] Eevee's name likely comes from the letters E-V, an abbreviation for the word "evolution". Its Japanese name, イーブイ( Eievui ), literally means "evolve". Eevee's original name, Eon, is simply the word eon (a long period of time). Names in other languages [ ] English, Italian, Portuguese and Spanish : Eevee French: Évoli German: Evoli Chinese: 伊布 / 伊貝 (HK) Korean: 이브 Reception [ ] Eevee with all eight of its evolved forms alongside Pikachu in the anime short Eevee &amp; Friends Due to Eevee's cute appearance and itself having many unique evolutions (commonly known as Eeveelutions ) unlike any other Pokémon which adds considerable replayability in the main games, it is widely seen among the Pokémon franchise 's community as a significant fan-favorite - rivaling if not surpassing that of Pikachu , the series' mascot. Although it technically has been popular since the original Generation I games where it debuted but was not initially promoted as heavily as certain other Pokémon, the Pokémon: Let's Go, Eevee! title of Generation VII as well as the anime special " Eevee &amp; Friends " had greatly increased its prominence among official media and is now one of the most iconic. Each of Eevee's own evolved forms are also well-beloved in their own right. Many have believed that this Pokémon should have been the franchise's overall mascot rather than Pikachu due to its adaptability in the games' lore reflecting the real-world conception of evolution and has been cited as a realistic interpretation of the phenomenon. Eevee is, however, currently serving as the role-playing video game series' secondary mascot. Eevee was ranked 20th place in the 2020 Pokémon of the Year poll, falling just behind Pikachu at 19th. Ironically, two of its evolved forms, Umbreon and Sylveon , were voted 5th and 6th, respectively. The official certification of Eevee Day in 2018 Since 2018, Eevee has an annual official day by The Pokémon Company where it and the Eeveelutions become nationally celebrated, known as Eevee Day. It takes place on November 21th (11/21), which its pronunciation in Goroawase (1121) is similar to Eevee's Japanese name " Eievui " and has been certified by the Japan Anniversary Association. A similar event in 2025 known as Year of Eevee occurred, where Eevee-themed limited edition merchandise are available. Despite this, fans have created unofficial dates for Eevee's separate evolved forms and even the evolution group itself on social media, leading to common misinformation. This problem is due to a wide misconception within the Pokémon fandom often claiming the day being solely for Eevee itself, as well as Pokémon GO being known to have hosted different community days for it and its evolutions. Trivia [ ] Eevee and Pikachu , as seen in Pokémon Yellow Version: Special Pikachu Edition and Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu! and Pokémon: Let's Go, Eevee! , are the only First partner Pokémon in the mainline RPG games that deviates from the usual Grass , Fire , and Water -typings. So far, Eevee is one of three Pokémon to have three or more evolutions, and is the only Pokémon that has four or more. The only other Pokémon with more than two evolutions are Tyrogue and Applin . Eevee was the first Pokémon to have a different evolution depending on the time of day, based on Espeon and Umbreon . Eevee's English name is a palindrome, meaning it is spelled the same backwards as is forward. Only four other Pokémon have this trait, those being Girafarig , Farigiraf , Ho-Oh , and Alomomola . Eevee, Poliwhirl and Gloom are the only three Pokémon that can evolve into more than one Pokémon using multiple elemental stones. An Eevee is bonded to the player character in Pokémon Conquest . In Pokémon the Series , specifically " The Rivalry Revival ", an Eevee owned by Gary Oak (which later become an Umbreon ) is shown defeating Ash's Pikachu in a battle, a homage to Blue 's ambiguous decision to have such a first partner Pokémon against the player's Pikachu in Pokémon Yellow . Eevee currently has by far the most evolutions of any Pokémon, with eight. Originally, in Generation I , there were only three - Flareon , Jolteon , and Vaporeon . Eevee is the Pokémon with the most evolutions using elemental stones so far, being three from Generation I to Generation VII and in Generation VIII being five. According to Game Freak , the reason why Eevee has not officially gained any new evolutions since Generation VI with Sylveon is due to the high amount of fan art of the otherwise unused types ( Ghost , Flying , Poison , Rock , Ground , Bug , Steel , and Dragon ) and their own being too similar to a fan's design, in spite of popular demand. They did attempt to do so for the Flying-type during the development of Pokémon Sun and Pokémon Moon but was scrapped. In the Eeveelution chain, all Pokémon evolving from Eevee have "eon" at the end of their names. It was the first Pokémon to be able to evolve by happiness in night or day, leveling up near the Moss Rock or Ice Rock up until Generation VIII, and having 2 affection hearts in Pokémon-Amie and knowing a Fairy -type move. In Pokémon Yellow , when the player attempts to have Eevee as their first partner Pokémon, Blue is seen reacting in surprise, and then charges and shoves the player character out of the way before taking it as his own, forcing the player to have Pikachu instead. All of Eevee's evolutions have unique cries based on its own. Up to Generation VIII, Eevee is one of the few Pokémon to evolve in a certain location (near a Moss Rock or a Ice Rock). The others being Magneton , Nosepass , Charjabug and Crabrawler . However, Eevee is the only one of them that has more than one evolution. Similarly, Eevee is one of the ten Pokémon that evolves upon knowing a move. In this case, it needs to know a Fairy -type move. Despite requiring a Fairy -type move to evolve into Sylveon, the only Fairy-type moves Eevee can learn are Baby-Doll Eyes and Charm through leveling up. Also in Pokémon Sun and Pokémon Moon , once the Eevee evolves into Sylveon, it can learn Fairy Wind . Eevee is so far the only Pokémon that didn't originally have a gender difference but received one in a later generation game . Up to Generation V , all generations include at least 2 versions where NPCs will give the player an Eevee. Eevee's original back sprite in Pocket Monsters Red Version and Pocket Monsters Green Version has its ears being oddly white-tipped, which is never a physical feature in official artwork. This would be corrected in Generation II . Out of every evolution of Eevee, only Flareon and Jolteon retain its fluffy mane, while the others lack such fur on their necks and Vaporeon has a white fin-like ruff. Some fans considered Flareon as Eevee's "natural" evolved form, as the Fire-type resembles it the most and also keeps the fluffy tail. In Pokémon FireRed Version , the Japanese Pokédex claims that Eevee have 3 evolutions while there are actually 5 in Generation III . The reason may be the lack of a time system in the game, which are necessary to evolve Eevee into Espeon or Umbreon. In the Super Smash Bros. series starting in the fourth installment , Eevee is one of the Pokémon that can be freed from Poké Balls . During this time, it periodically uses the move Take Down against any nearby fighters. Alongside Eevee, Motofumi Fujiwara is also the creator of Jolteon and Flareon. The rest of the Eeveelutions were designed by Atsuko Nishida , who also created Pikachu. Eevee's English name was originally going to be Eon , which is used for the end of the name of each of its eight evolutions. Eevee and its evolutions' official celebration day on November 21st, Eevee Day, coincidentally shares the same date as the Super Nintendo Entertainment System 's original Japanese release in 1990 (known in the region as Super Famicom) as well as two early titles from The Legend of Zelda series - A Link to the Past and Ocarina of Time . In Pokémon Yellow , as Blue uses Eevee as his first partner Pokémon, it is possible for him to evolve it into the three available Eeveelutions at the time based on the player's battle results against him. If they defeat Blue at both Prof. Oak's lab and Route 22 , it will become a Jolteon. If they lose or skip the optional Route 22 battle, it becomes a Flareon. If the player loses at the lab, however, it instead becomes a Vaporeon. This unique balance of difficulty marks the first time the player character can have a type advantage over the Rival 's based on their Pikachu. The first battle at the laboratory is infamously known to be rather challenging compared to the initial Red and Blue games, as Pikachu has poor Defense stats while Eevee has Tackle and Tail Whip as its moves, which can be problematic had it lands any Critical Hits or never uses the latter attack. Eevee makes a cameo appearance as a special limited-time furniture in Animal Crossing: Pocket Camp of the Animal Crossing series , alongside Mario , Splatoon , and Sanrio's Hello Kitty franchises. It is specifically for the Poké Ball Scavenger Hunt event which took place between October 12 to 23, 2018, as part of the first Eevee Day celebration. However, they are no longer legitimately available in the Complete version. Eevee was chosen as Pikachu's counterpart in Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu! and Pokémon: Let's Go, Eevee! primarily due to its popularity and also its simplicity. In Pokémon Stadium 2 , this Pokémon stars in the Eager Eevee mini-game. Until the Let's Go games, the only way the player could legitimately obtain an Eevee in a Pokémon game primarily set in Kanto was to get the one in the secret entrance in Celadon Mansion. In the Nintendo Switch titles, it was added to Route 17 . Eevee is the Generation I first partner Pokémon least officially depicted with the others in most Pokémon media. Surprisingly, despite Penny 's, the villainous team leader of Team Star , admiration of Eevee, she does not own the Pokémon itself on her team, but rather all of its evolutions (which she calls them "Veevees"). The bag she wears, however, is based on Eevee. Her own team being almost solely Eeveelutions is a mirror to players who collect multiple Eevee to obtain the full set available in the core series . Eevee got its cry from the anime (voiced by Aoi Yūki ) in all games after Pokémon Let's Go , a feature that was previously exclusive to Pikachu. Partner Eevee as seen in Pokémon Let's Go, Eevee! , featuring unique violet eyes and a heart-tipped tail The Partner Eevee in Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu! and Pokémon: Let's Go, Eevee! is Shiny-locked. This means that this particular Eevee cannot be a Shiny Pokémon . Its model is slightly larger and significantly more detailed than its standard counterparts seen in the wild or as Trace 's first partner Pokémon in Let's Go, Pikachu! . It's also known to have an advantage over Partner Pikachu, as it has more Exclusive Moves and each one are based on every type of its evolved forms. In " Welcome to Naranja Academy! ", it shows the Terastallized Eevee with its Trainer of the student at the Naranja Academy . Like the Z-Moves , the Gigantamax move, and the Terastallized of Eevee, it is unknown that it'll have more Z-Crystals , Gigantamax moves, and the Terastallized for the eight evolved forms of Eevee.
