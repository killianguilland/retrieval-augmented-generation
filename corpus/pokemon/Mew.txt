Mew, a Mythical Pokémon, can be legally acquired through Pokémon distribution events or by transferring from the Poké Ball Plus to games like Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu!, Pokémon: Let's Go, Eevee!, Pokémon Sword, or Pokémon Shield. In Pokémon Diamond or Pearl, you can get Mew by transferring 999 Pokémon and 1 egg to My Pokémon Ranch. Additionally, Mew can be obtained in Pokémon Ranger and transferred to any Generation IV game. Provided by: Fandom What was Mew's DNA used for in the Pokémon universe? Mew's DNA, in the Pokémon universe, was utilized to create Mewtwo, a Psychic Pokémon. Mew, despite being the genetic template for Mewtwo, is listed after Mewtwo in the Pokédex. Mew is believed to carry the genetic code of all Pokémon, a theory backed by its unique ability to learn Transform, a trait shared by only two other Pokémon. Provided by: Fandom What physical characteristics distinguish Mew from other Pokémon? Mew, a Pokémon, is characterized by its pink hair, long thin tail with a large bulge, and large three-toed feet. It has short arms with three fingers on each paw and small, orange paw pads. Despite its power, Mew exhibits a playful and mischievous nature. Its unique genetic composition allows it to use all known Pokémon moves. Provided by: Fandom Is Mew considered a cat-like Pokémon? Mew, a cat-like Pokémon, possesses small, pointed ears, blue eyes, a long tail, and large three-toed feet. Its pink fur is fine and almost invisible without a microscope. The name 'Mew' is likely derived from 'meow', a common cat sound, further emphasizing its feline nature. Provided by: Fandom Biology [ ] Physiology [ ] Concept artwork of Mew. Mew is a small, distinctly cat-like Pokémon with blunt, pointed ears, vivid blue eyes, a rounded embryo-like body-shape, and a long, thin tail with a large bulge at the end. Its arms are small and have three fingers each, while its feet are somewhat long with three toes. Orange paw pads are on the undersides of its feet. It is covered with a layer of fine, pink fur, only visible with the aid of a microscope. Behavior [ ] Despite itself being one of the most powerful Pokémon in the world , Mew is very playful and mischievous. It can get along with many different humans and Pokémon and will risk its life for its friends. However, Mew is very shy and will only show itself to those pure of heart. It is also one of the most intelligent, as it is very adapt at battles and solving problems, as seen in the anime series . In the games, Faraway Island is where Mew resides, though this place is exclusively seen in Pokémon Emerald Version when obtaining the Old Sea Map - an event item only distributed to Japan. Here, it plays hide and seek inside tall grass before the player can encounter and capture the Mythical Pokémon. Characteristics [ ] It is said that Mew's DNA possesses the genetic composition of every existing Pokémon species, thus allowing it to use all known Pokémon moves (and Pokémon's exclusive Z-Moves , excluding Ultra Necrozma's Z-Move). Its possession of every Pokémon's DNA also allows it to de-evolve Pokémon, as shown in the Trading Card Game through its moves "Devolution Beam" and "Devo Crush". Evolution [ ] Mew does not evolve . Trivia [ ] Mew was the very first Pokémon trademark ever to be applied for and registered in the franchise . In some media, Mew is considered the rarest of all Pokémon. Mew is one of the most popular Mythical Pokémon among the franchise's community, with many calling it among the cutest yet powerful Pokémon. Mew is said to hold the DNA of all Pokémon. Mew and Ditto have very similar normal and Shiny colors - pink and sky blue, respectively. In Pokemon: The First Movie , Mew's reason for battling Mewtwo aside from defending itself was to tell its counterpart that a Pokemon's strength does not come from having special powers but rather it comes from the heart. In the original Japanese version, however, it also wanted to protect original uncloned Pokémon, believing them to be just as good, if not better than the clones created by Mewtwo. In Mewtwo Returns , although Mew does not play a major role unlike in its prequel, it can be briefly seen flying past Mewtwo when its counterpart was healing inside Clarity Lake . All later Mythical Pokémon share the same stats as Mew: a base total of 600, with the exception of Phione , Arceus , Keldeo , and Meltan . Despite Mew's DNA being used to create Mewtwo , Mew comes after Mewtwo in the Pokédex , with Mew being #151 and Mewtwo being #150. Therefore, it is the last Pokémon of Generation I . The film Lucario and the Mystery of Mew is based solely upon Mew alongside Lucario . Despite being labelled "the ancestor of all Pokémon" it is unknown whether or not Mew was the first Pokémon to exist in the Pokémon World . It was the first that was trademarked, but story-wise, Arceus is said to have been alone when it was born and it itself created other Pokémon. Although it can learn all moves, Mew cannot learn rare moves such as Blast Burn , Judgment , Secret Sword and Freeze Shock , mainly because certain moves are to be learned by a specific Pokémon, and Mew cannot learn them under a specific condition. In spite of having a feminine-looking appearance, Mew's gender is unknown. It is the only known Pokémon to have its DNA used in a cloning process to create a different Pokémon: Mewtwo was created by cloning and gene splicing, Team Rocket (who were behind the experiments) were planning on creating a "Mewthree". However, this plan failed when Mewtwo destroyed the lab. In Japan, a pink Nintendo DS was released with an image of Mew on it. In the original Pokémon games, Mew was only officially available through distribution events running during 1999-2000 outside Japan where such events ran approximately 4 years before. As these events are long since discontinued, it is impossible to complete the Pokédex or at least obtain Mew, for Generation I games without performing the Mew Glitch or using a memory modification device like the GameShark or Action Replay. Mew was the first event Pokémon introduced. Mew wasn't originally going to be an actual Pokémon; it was initially just a prank set up by one of Red/Blue ' s developers S higeki Morimoto , and behind the creators' backs after the game was finished during development. However, due to all the publicity it got, Mew became official. Because Mewtwo was technically designed first, Mew was based on its design, but simplified. In celebration of Pokémon's 20th anniversary in 2016, a Mew could be obtained via mystery gift at select GameStop stores on February 1 - February 24 for Pokémon X/Y or Omega Ruby/Alpha Sapphire . In Australia, EB Games is still giving out Mew codes. Despite being canonically very powerful, Mew's wild encounter at Faraway Island has the Mythical Pokémon only at level 30. Mew placed 3rd in a special poll created to see which Pokémon was the most popular to be distributed for the movie, Volcanion and the Mechanical Marvel . In the mini-series Pokémon Origins , Mew was the only Pokémon of the original 151 that Red was unable to catch. At the end of Episode 4, he explained to Prof. Oak and Blue that Mewtwo was cloned from an extremely rare Pokémon, and deduced in excitement that the original must still exist somewhere; as he says this, Mew is seen flying outside the window, smiling. Mew is tied with Muk for the shortest English Pokémon name (3 letters). Obtaining a Shiny Mew in Generation II is technically impossible without hacking, since Mew was first given out in Generation I (when Shiny Pokémon did not yet exist). Mew is one of the few Pokémon with equally balanced stats (all of its stats are 100). Currently, Cryogonal , Minior &amp; Mew are the only gender unknown Pokémon that can learn Attract (although they can only learn Attract via TM ), where the move always fails. In the Super Smash Bros. series, Mew is one of the Pokémon that can be freed from Poké Balls . During this time, it only uses the move Fly . Mew is the only Generation I Pokémon that has a medium-slow experience growth rate and is not part of a three-stage evolutionary family. In the Pokemon Stadium games, Mew can be chosen for rentals for R-2 mode when the player completes the main game. Older official artwork depicts Mew having slightly darker-colored fur around its toes. Mew's mouth, similar to Espeon , is hidden under its muzzle. Origin [ ] Mew is based on a cat (more accurately a kitten). It also looks similar to a vertebrate embryo , specifically a feline embryo. Etymology [ ] Mew's name seems to be derived from the words "meow" and literally "mew", the sound a cat or kitten makes, and "mutant", as well as possibly a corruption of “new”, considering it is the New Species Pokémon.
