Mewtwo, a Psychic type Pokémon, was artificially created by scientists in the Pokémon Mansion. It made its debut in Pokémon Red, Green, and Blue, and is found in Cerulean Cave post becoming the Kanto Region Champion. Despite being cloned from Mew, Mewtwo precedes Mew in the Pokédex. Its name is derived from 'Mew', the Pokémon it was cloned from, and 'two', suggesting it's a modern iteration of Mew. Provided by: Fandom What are the two forms that Mewtwo can Mega Evolve into? Mewtwo, a notable Pokémon, can undergo Mega Evolution into two distinct forms: Mega Mewtwo X and Mega Mewtwo Y. The transformation requires Mewtwonite X for Mega Mewtwo X and Mewtwonite Y for Mega Mewtwo Y. Mega Mewtwo X, characterized by stronger limbs, improves Mewtwo's fighting prowess, while Mega Mewtwo Y, recognized by its slender head, enhances its psychic abilities. Provided by: Fandom What additional type does Mega Mewtwo X gain upon evolution? Mega Mewtwo X, an evolution of Mewtwo, acquires an extra Fighting-type. This transformation, triggered by Mewtwonite X, not only alters Mewtwo's physical form but also boosts its combat abilities. Provided by: Fandom How does Mewtwo's appearance differ from that of Mew? Mewtwo, a feline-like Pokémon, exhibits a mutated humanoid appearance in contrast to Mew. It has a purplish-gray and light purple body, three fingers per hand, and two toes per foot. Mew's ears are replaced by horn-like extrusions in Mewtwo. Unique forms such as Armored Mewtwo and Mega Mewtwo augment Mewtwo's psychic abilities and attack statistics. In the anime series, Mewtwo features prominently in Mewtwo Strikes Back and Mewtwo Returns. Provided by: Fandom Biology [ ] Physiology [ ] Mewtwo is a bipedal, humanoid feline-like Pokémon . Its appearance is loosely based on Mew , with a mutated, significantly more human-like look in addition to its feline traits, due to horrific gene-splicing experiments. Its body is purplish gray and light purple, It has three round digits on each paw, with only two front digits on its feet, and one supporting round heel-like digit at the back. It has horn-like protrusions on its head. Mewtwo projects a faint but powerful purple aura when at full power, though it appears blue in Pokémon the Series . Forms [ ] Armored Mewtwo Clefairy transformed into Mewthree Mewtwo has an exclusive form not seen in the main games named 'Armored Mewtwo' in the anime, and 'M2 Bind' in the manga . In the anime series, specifically Mewtwo Strikes Back , the form increases all of Mewtwo's Psychic abilities (despite Mewtwo's claims it suppresses his strength), and in the manga it allows Giovanni to control it. Mewtwo also has another form exclusive to the Pocket Monsters manga, where it takes on the DNA of a Clefairy , becoming a shorter, fatter and much stronger version of itself. Titling itself "Mewthree", it started its quest of cooking the best bacon ever until Satoshi managed to calm it down with his bacon. After going into a tantrum, screaming out " This isn't even my final form! ", Mewthree split back into Clefairy and Mewtwo. Characteristics [ ] An artificial Pokémon specifically designed for the fiercest battles , Mewtwo is highly adept at utilizing its Psychic powers, displaying powerful telekinesis and telepathy as well. It speaks in a masculine voice in most Pokémon media, and is generally masculine in build (despite being unknown gender in-game). It utilizes its telepathy to communicate with humans and translate the Pokémon language. It uses its telekinesis to skillfully fly, block special abilities, and expunge people's recollections (make others forget things) as optically discerned in Mewtwo Strikes Back and Mewtwo Returns . Behavior [ ] As evidence of its tremendous power surpassing even that of Mew's, Mewtwo is by far one of the most domineering Pokémon in the franchise. Aside from being among the most powerful Pokémon in the core series (especially in Generation I where its Special stat is quite high and the Psychic-type in general having few weaknesses), in the anime , its strength is so great to the point that it can endure immense torture in its sequel movie Mewtwo Returns , and teleport a lake miles deep underground. Proven in the first Pokémon film, its official debut in Mewtwo Strikes Back , Mewtwo holds a large degree of pride in its personality. Its general nature is so complex that it borderlines that of human levels with itself, capable of learning and evolving just to get the upper hand in battle. It is shown to at first be furious toward humans due to its early traumatic experiences caused by the evil Team Rocket and scientists and Giovanni creating it as a mere clone and weapon, and not a natural Pokémon born of Mew . Later on, it becomes more underanding and kind, learning that not all humans are evil, and actually secretly protecting them, and its fellow clone Pokémon. In the core series, it is documented by the Team Rocket scientists that Mewtwo was born of Mew (in a document in a secret abandoned laboratory within Cinnabar Island that reads "Mew gave birth" to it). It however became too violent and uncontrollable, and fled to the treacherous Cerulean Cave , far away from humans, to live in solitude. Although not technically invincible, Mewtwo proves to be an extremely formidable and powerful Legendary Pokémon with a savage personality in stark contrast to Mew, unwilling to be captured, with a particularly low catch-rate in the games. Despite this, it can be captured and tamed, like any other Pokémon. Personality [ ] In the games, because it is known to have the most savage heart among all Pokémon in polar opposite to Mew (but is not evil) due to its artificial gene manipulation. Mewtwo is generally shown as a stoic or fearless Legendary Pokémon, but when tamed by the player character , it can grow to care for him/her, though it takes a longer amount of time than most other Pokémon. Unlike in the anime and manga, Mewtwo does not speak in the franchise's main media. In Pokémon the Series , Mewtwo is highly intelligent - more so than most Pokémon, and even humans. In Pokémon: The First Movie where it serves as the main antagonist. It started out as an extremely arrogant, ruthless, cold and aggressive Pokémon, being asocial, violent, and gruff, disliking humans with a passion due to how it was treated as a clone and weapon by the evil Team Rocket; it tended to smirk in a smug manner when interacting with trainers and their non-cloned Pokémon. Later on, after learning that not all humans are evil, it is shown to be willing to help others. An example of its compassion are events in which it assisted Ash and his companions, and saved a bus full of other humans from a catastrophe with its psychic powers. When one of the Pokémon clones it was protecting asked about this and suggested that Mewtwo was growing fond of humans, Mewtwo quickly dismissed the idea, though it was proven to be in denial of it growing compassionate tendencies. It progressively proved to be truly heroic and kind, as seen in Mewtwo Returns and Pokemon Journeys: The Series . In Mewtwo Strikes Back , alongside being able to create and organize a seemingly global hurricane , Mewtwo is smart enough to build a rather advanced castle of its own on New Island with a fully functional cloning factory producing Mewtwo Balls - custom sinister-looking Poké Balls which can capture Pokémon even if already inside their own legitimate Poké Ball. Evolution [ ] Mewtwo does not evolve , but can Mega Evolve into either Mega Mewtwo X using its Mewtwonite X or Mega Mewtwo Y using its Mewtwonite Y . Mega Mewtwo X adds an additional Fighting -type to it. Trivia [ ] Mewtwo has been ranked 8th on Kanto 's Pokemon of the Year 2020 poll. Mewtwo has been noted as being the most powerful Pokémon in existence. The most recent note of this was in Mewtwo - Prologue to Awakening , where it is stated twice. Mewtwo is the only Pokémon thus far to be created via cloning , although not the only one to be created and/or modified scientifically. Some other examples are Porygon , Castform and Genesect . Mewtwo is the first Pokémon to receive a Mega Evolution , as well being one of three Pokémon possessing two different Mega Evolutions, along with Charizard , Raichu , Absol , Garchomp , and Lucario . Mewtwo is the first Legendary Pokémon to have a Mega Evolution. With the ability to mega evolve , Mewtwo gains the highest Pokémon (alongside Rayquaza ) with a base stat total of 780. Counting the original Mewtwo from the original anime series along with the Best Wishes Mewtwo, and due to the events of Pokémon Ranger: Guardian Sign s , there are three known Mewtwo in the Pokémon World . In all previous episodes and movies, Mewtwo's voice is heard as male, but in Mewtwo — Prologue to Awakening and Genesect and the Legend Awakened , Mewtwo's voice is female. Despite this, in the games, Mewtwo's gender is considered unknown. In Pokémon Stadium , Pokémon Puzzle Challenge , and Pokkén Tournament , Mewtwo is depicted as the games' Final Boss . Despite being humanoid and standing 6'7" tall in height, Mewtwo's size, like most other Pokémon, is inconsistent across official media. According to The Official Pokemon Handbook, defeating or capturing Mewtwo automatically makes a Pokémon Trainer become a Pokémon Master . Contrary to hints in the mainline games that Mewtwo is only capturable in a Master Ball , it is possible to capture Mewtwo in a standard Poké Ball . Mega Mewtwo X has the highest Attack stat at 190 while Mega Mewtwo Y has the highest Special Attack stat at 194. In Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu! and Pokémon: Let's Go, Eevee! , when using the Master Ball against Mewtwo, it can be seen swatting the item away from itself, though it is still captured despite this in-game animation. It is a playable fighter in the Super Smash Bros. series, starting with Melee . Mewtwo's castle in Pokémon: The First Movie has it depicted in the eye of a cyclone According to the original Japanese version of Mewtwo Strikes Back , the hurricane-like storm was a test by Mewtwo for skilled Trainers to reach New Island and enter its castle, whereas in the international release, it claims it is powerful enough to destroy the entire Pokémon World. Mewtwo was created from Mew's DNA story-wise; though during the development of Pokémon Red Version and Pokémon Green Version , it was designed first, and Mew's own design was based on Mewtwo itself, but rather simplified. In the alternate lore of the Pokémon Adventures manga, some human cells were used to create Mewtwo, specifically from Blaine , a Team Rocket scientist. This gave Baine a psychic connection with the Pokémon, enough to be able to track it. This is not part of Mewtwo's creation in any other form of Pokémon media. Origin [ ] Like Mew, Mewtwo is based on a cat embryo , though anthropomorphic with elements of gray aliens , and a tail and paws slightly similar to salamanders . Etymology [ ] Mewtwo and Myūtsū come from the name of the Mythical Pokémon from which it was cloned, " Mew ", and "two", referring to how its DNA is similar to Mew and could be considered its modern predecessor.
